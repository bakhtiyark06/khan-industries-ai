# Khan Industries — Services Strategy Manual

This document is the **single source of truth for every professional service Khan Industries offers, how those services are delivered, and how they evolve over time**.

It governs how consultants, engineers, sales contributors, and AI assistants scope, propose, build, deploy, and support client work. This is an **internal company standard** — not marketing copy. Customer-facing materials must be derived from this manual, not the other way around.

If a service engagement conflicts with this manual, **this manual wins** until it is deliberately updated.

> A service is not a task list. A service is a **structured engagement that delivers measurable business value** — built with premium quality, honest scope, and long-term accountability.

## 1. Service Philosophy

Khan Industries delivers **premium technology services** — custom, hands-on work that helps businesses save time, increase revenue, reduce operational complexity, improve customer experience, and scale intelligently.

Services are distinct from products (`02_PRODUCTS.md`). Products are scalable, repeatable software. Services are tailored engagements for individual clients with defined scope, deliverables, and accountability.

Our service philosophy rests on four convictions:

1. **Measurable value.** Every service must create business value the client can recognize — not activity for its own sake. If it does not map to at least one of the five value outcomes below, it is not offered.
2. **Premium at every scope.** Starter engagements receive the same quality standards as Enterprise engagements. Scope differs; quality does not.
3. **Honesty in capability.** We offer services we can deliver well. We do not claim expertise, certifications, or past delivery we do not have.
4. **Partnership over transactions.** Services are designed to begin long-term relationships, not one-off jobs that end at deployment.

**The five value outcomes** (from `00_COMPANY.md`):

1. Save time
2. Increase revenue
3. Reduce operational complexity (operational stress)
4. Improve customer experience (quality)
5. Scale intelligently (make systems easier to run)

**The service test:** before any service is proposed or scoped, it must clearly support one or more of these outcomes. If it does not, it should not be sold.

**Company position:** Khan Industries is an early-stage technology company. Services are **defined and offered** as part of the company's professional capabilities. This manual does not claim prior client delivery, case studies, or certifications unless they are documented elsewhere with evidence.

## 5. Client Engagement Model

Every client engagement follows a defined journey. Stages may compress for small Starter scopes but must not be skipped in substance.

```
Discovery
    ↓
Consultation
    ↓
Proposal
    ↓
Development
    ↓
Testing
    ↓
Deployment
    ↓
Training
    ↓
Support
    ↓
Long-term Partnership
```

| Stage | Purpose | Key Activities | Exit Criteria |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Discovery** | Understand the client's business, pain, and constraints. | Intake questions; current systems review; goal alignment to value outcomes. | Problem and success criteria documented; fit confirmed. |
| **Consultation** | Explore solutions honestly; no overselling. | Recommendations; scope options; feasibility and timeline realism. | Client understands options, trade-offs, and what Khan Industries can deliver. |
| **Proposal** | Formalize scope, deliverables, timeline, and pricing tier. | Written proposal; statement of work; assumptions and exclusions explicit. | Client acceptance or negotiated revision. |
| **Development** | Build to `05_ENGINEERING.md` and service-specific standards. | Iterative build; client checkpoints; change control for scope shifts. | Feature-complete per agreed scope. |
| **Testing** | Verify quality before production exposure. | QA; UAT with client; security and performance baseline. | Sign-off or documented issue resolution plan. |
| **Deployment** | Release to production or live environment. | Deployment runbook; rollback plan; communication to stakeholders. | Live system meeting acceptance criteria. |
| **Training** | Enable client to operate and own outcomes. | Walkthroughs; documentation; admin training where applicable. | Client confirms understanding of operation and support channels. |
| **Support** | Stabilize post-launch; fix defects in scope. | Hypercare window; bug fixes; monitoring as agreed. | Stable operation; transition to maintenance or partnership. |
| **Long-term Partnership** | Ongoing value through retainers, products, or new projects. | Check-ins; optimization; upsell only when genuinely valuable. | Renewed engagement or product subscription. |

**Engagement principles:**

- Discovery and consultation are not bait for oversized proposals — scope must match real need.
- Changes after proposal require written change order or revised proposal.
- Long-term partnership is the default intent; one-off delivery without support handoff is the exception and must be explicit.

## 6. Pricing Philosophy

Khan Industries uses three **engagement levels** — Starter, Growth, and Enterprise — aligned with `01_BRAND.md`. **No dollar amounts are defined in this manual**; pricing is set per proposal based on scope, value, and tier.

| Level | Who It Serves | What It Represents | Typical Scope Characteristics |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Starter** | Smaller organizations or first engagements with Khan Industries. | Premium quality at focused scope — an entry point that still reflects full standards. | Single primary deliverable (e.g., landing page, one chatbot flow, one automation); shorter timeline; defined boundaries. |
| **Growth** | Scaling businesses needing integration and ongoing improvement. | Deeper systems, multiple deliverables, stronger connection between services. | Multiple service categories; integrations; phased delivery; optional retainer. |
| **Enterprise** | Organizations requiring scale, reliability, and partnership. | Tailored architecture, rigorous process, dedicated attention, long-term alignment. | Complex systems; compliance and security considerations; SLAs as agreed; strategic advisory component. |

**Pricing rules:**

- **Value-based framing.** Price ties to outcomes (time saved, revenue enabled, complexity reduced) — not hourly rate shopping alone.
- **Transparent structure.** Proposals list what is included, excluded, and what triggers additional scope.
- **Never cheapest.** Compete on quality, reliability, and trust — not on being the lowest bid.
- **Every tier is premium.** Starter is not discount quality; it is appropriately scoped premium work.
- **Products vs. services.** Recurring product subscriptions are priced per product strategy (`02_PRODUCTS.md`); custom services are priced per engagement.

**Internal note:** Specific rate cards, packages, and templates may be maintained outside this manual (e.g., sales ops or `07_SALES.md`) but must honor this philosophy.

## 7. Service Standards

Every current service must meet these cross-cutting standards in addition to the per-service definitions in Section 3.

### 7.1 Universal Requirements

| Standard | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| **Scope clarity** | Written scope before development begins; assumptions and exclusions visible. |
| **Brand alignment** | Client-facing deliverables that represent Khan Industries craftsmanship meet `01_BRAND.md` where applicable. |
| **Engineering alignment** | Technical work follows `05_ENGINEERING.md` — TypeScript preference, maintainable structure, production readiness. |

| **Honesty** | Capabilities and timelines stated accurately; no implied prior delivery or fake social proof. |
| **Documentation** | Handoff materials sufficient for another engineer or the client to understand the system. |
| **Security baseline** | Secrets not exposed; authentication considered; data handling discussed in discovery. |

### 7.2 Deliverable Quality Bar

A service deliverable is not complete when it "works once." It is complete when it:

- Meets agreed acceptance criteria.
- Handles common errors gracefully.
- Is deployable or transferable per the proposal.
- Has been reviewed against Section 8 (Quality Assurance).

### 7.3 Roles (Early-Stage)

At early stage, roles may be consolidated (e.g., founder as consultant, engineer, and account owner). As the company grows, roles split — but **standards do not relax**.

## 8. Quality Assurance

Quality assurance is mandatory before deployment. The depth of QA scales with engagement level and risk.

### 8.1 QA Checklist (Baseline)

- [ ] Deliverables match signed scope and proposal.
- [ ] Functional testing on primary user paths completed.
- [ ] Responsive behavior verified (for web deliverables).
- [ ] Accessibility baseline considered (keyboard, contrast, labels where applicable).
- [ ] No critical security issues (exposed keys, open endpoints without auth where required).
- [ ] Client UAT completed or documented waiver for low-risk internal tools.
- [ ] Documentation and handoff package prepared.

### 8.2 QA by Service Type

| Service Type | Additional QA Focus |
| --- | --- |
| **AI Agents / Chatbots** | Out-of-scope handling; escalation paths; prompt/injection baseline review; transparency that user is interacting with AI. |
| **Websites** | Performance; SEO technical basics; cross-browser smoke test; content accuracy. |
| **Automation / APIs** | Failure modes; retries; logging; idempotency where relevant. |
| **Cloud & MLOps** | Rollback tested; monitoring alerts fire; environment parity documented. |
| **Dashboards** | Metric validation against source data; permission boundaries. |

### 8.3 Release Authority

Nothing deploys to production for a client without explicit readiness confirmation — internal sign-off at minimum, client sign-off where agreed.

**Rule:** Never deploy untested systems to client production environments.

## 9. Maintenance Philosophy

Delivery is not the end of accountability. Maintenance expectations must be set in the proposal.

### 9.1 Tiers of Ongoing Support

| Tier | Description | Typical Inclusion |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hypercare** | Short post-launch window included in project. | Bug fixes for in-scope defects; stability monitoring; rapid response. |
| **Retainer** | Ongoing monthly or quarterly agreement. | Small enhancements; monitoring; priority support; advisory time. |
| **Ad-hoc** | Pay-per-request after hypercare. | Best-effort scheduling; scoped quotes per request. |
| **Product-backed** | Client uses a Khan Industries product with subscription support. | Per product terms in `02_PRODUCTS.md`. |

### 9.2 What Maintenance Includes

- Fixing defects in delivered scope.
- Keeping dependencies and hosting viable (when maintenance agreement covers infrastructure).
- Agreed minor updates and content changes within retainer hours.

### 9.3 What Maintenance Excludes (Unless Re-Scoped)

- New features outside original scope.
- Third-party platform price increases or policy changes outside Khan Industries control.
- Issues caused by client modifications without coordination.

### 9.4 Philosophy

Khan Industries **does not disappear after delivery**. Every engagement ends with clear support expectations — even if that expectation is "ad-hoc only after 30-day hypercare." Ambiguity erodes trust.

## 10. Customer Success Philosophy

Customer success means the client **achieves the business outcomes** the engagement was designed for — not merely that the project shipped.

### 10.1 Principles

1. **Outcomes over outputs.** A deployed chatbot that nobody uses is a failure; a simpler bot that deflects 40% of tier-1 tickets is a success.
2. **Proactive communication.** Clients should never wonder what is happening on their project.
3. **Education empowers.** Training and documentation reduce dependency and increase satisfaction.
4. **Honest measurement.** Success metrics from Section 3 are reviewed with the client where data exists — without inventing results.
5. **Expansion when earned.** Upsell additional services or products only when they clearly serve the client's stated goals.

### 10.2 Success Review

For Growth and Enterprise engagements, schedule a **30-day post-launch review**:

- What is working?
- What metrics moved (if measurable)?
- What friction remains?
- Is retainer or phase two appropriate?

### 10.3 Closing Standard

Every engagement should support the brand closing feeling from `01_BRAND.md`:

> *"I made the right decision choosing Khan Industries."*

## 12. What Khan Industries Will Never Do

These boundaries protect clients, the brand, and the company's long-term reputation.

### What We Will Never Do

- **Never overpromise.** Scope, timelines, and outcomes stated honestly — including what AI cannot do.
- **Never fake expertise.** No claimed certifications, client logos, or case studies that do not exist.
- **Never deploy untested systems.** Production releases require QA per Section 8.
- **Never sacrifice security.** Secrets, access control, and data handling treated seriously even on small projects.
- **Never disappear after delivery.** Support expectations are explicit; ghosting is unacceptable.
- **Never deliver poor-quality work.** Premium standards apply at every engagement level.
- **Never sell services that violate values.** No deceptive, harmful, or manipulative systems for clients.
- **Never confuse products with custom scope.** Product subscriptions are not disguised as unlimited custom development.
- **Never use fake urgency or hidden fees.** Pricing philosophy from Section 6 and `01_BRAND.md` applies to all proposals.

### What We Will Always Do

- **Map engagements to measurable value outcomes.**
- **Document scope and deliverables before build.**
- **Hand off with clarity** — documentation, training, and support path.
- **Learn from every engagement** to improve products and playbooks.

## How Services Connect to Products

| Connection | Service Role |
